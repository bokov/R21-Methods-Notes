Analysis

We will calculate the surgical administrative risk assessment index (RAI-A) for patient records in the VASQIP \cite{hall_development_2017} and NSQIP  \cite{isharwal_preoperative_2017,melin_preoperative_2015} registries of STVHS and UHS, respectively. This RAI-A score will be used as the predictor to fit Cox proportional hazard models \cite{cox_regression_1972} for time from surgery until first unplanned re-admission and until mortality. We will update these models with the following additional variables: race, gender, Hispanic/Latino ethnicity, and median household income and use bi-directional stepwise regression \cite{venables_modern_2002} to find a parsimonious set of demographic covariates that significantly improve predictive accuracy.
